## What Is Paper Trading?

[[Lexicon#paper trading|Paper trading]] refers to simulated trading ‚Äì practicing buying and selling financial instruments without using real money. In a paper trading account (also called a **trading simulator** or demo account), you make trades with *virtual* funds, so there‚Äôs no real monetary risk[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/trading/thinkorswim/paper-trading#:~:text=paperMoney%C2%AE%20is%20the%20virtual%20trading,during%20live%20trading%20within%20thinkorswim). This concept dates back to when learners would jot down imaginary trades on paper, but today it‚Äôs done through sophisticated online platforms that mimic real market conditions[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/4-reasons-to-try-paper-trading#:~:text=The%20term%20,Paper%20trading%20at%20Schwab). In essence, paper trading lets you **practice investing in real-time markets with zero risk**, since only ‚Äúimaginary‚Äù money is at stake[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/trading/thinkorswim/paper-trading#:~:text=paperMoney%C2%AE%20is%20the%20virtual%20trading,during%20live%20trading%20within%20thinkorswim).

## Why Practice with a Trading Simulator?

Using a trading simulator offers several benefits for new and experienced investors alike:

- **No Financial Risk:** You can‚Äôt lose real money. This allows you to test strategies and learn from mistakes without fear of financial loss[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/4-reasons-to-try-paper-trading#:~:text=I%27ve%20found%20the%20most%20effective,without%20the%20threat%20of%20losses)[investopedia.com](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/active-trading/072915/pros-and-cons-paper-trading.asp#:~:text=No%20Risk). For a first-time trader, dipping your toes in with paper trades helps build confidence without the ‚Äúthreat of losses‚Äù hanging over you[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/4-reasons-to-try-paper-trading#:~:text=I%27ve%20found%20the%20most%20effective,without%20the%20threat%20of%20losses).
- **Real Market Conditions:** Quality simulators use live or near-live market data, so you can see how the markets are changing and how your investments would perform in actual conditions[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/trading/thinkorswim/paper-trading#:~:text=paperMoney%C2%AE%20is%20the%20virtual%20trading,during%20live%20trading%20within%20thinkorswim). You get to observe real price movements, volatility, and news impacts in real time, but in a sandbox environment.
- **Skill Development:** It‚Äôs a chance to refine your trading skills and test new strategies. You can try out different trade types or complex orders and see the outcomes. Treating the practice as if it were real ‚Äì doing research, setting entry/exit points, and monitoring positions ‚Äì helps you develop good habits[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/4-reasons-to-try-paper-trading#:~:text=,improve%20your%20craft%20is%20by). Any flaws in your strategy or understanding will show up in the results, which you can then study and learn from.
- **Emotional Prep:** Trading real money can trigger emotions like fear and greed that affect decision-making. While a simulator can‚Äôt fully reproduce the psychological pressure of real gains or losses, it lets you get a feel for following a plan without those pressures[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/4-reasons-to-try-paper-trading#:~:text=That%20said%2C%20be%20mindful%20of,gain%20experience%20without%20the%20risk). As you gain experience in the simulator, you‚Äôll hopefully be better prepared to manage reactions when you eventually trade live.

*(Tip: To get the most out of paper trading, approach it seriously. Use the same amount of money and types of trades you would in real life and keep a log of your trades and thought processes. This way, you build realistic experience and avoid developing bad habits in the ‚Äúplay‚Äù environment[schwab.com](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/4-reasons-to-try-paper-trading#:~:text=,improve%20your%20craft%20is%20by).)*
